The Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary, also known as Thattekad Bird Sanctuary, is a heaven for birdwatchers located in Kerala, India. It's named after renowned Indian ornithologist Dr. Salim Ali and covers an area of around 25 square kilometers along the banks of the Periyar River. The sanctuary is home to over 300 species of birds, making it one of the richest bird habitats in India. Some common bird species found here include the Malabar Grey Hornbill, Crested Serpent Eagle, Kingfisher, and many migratory waterbirds like the Pintail Duck and Greater Flamingo. The sanctuary is also a significant habitat for various other wildlife including reptiles, amphibians, and insects.
