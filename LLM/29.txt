The Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS) are a set of accounting standards developed by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) with the objective of enhancing the quality, reliability, comparability and transparency of financial reporting in India. They aim to establish a single set of high-quality, globally comparable accounting standards for all or specific types of entities that choose to adopt them on a voluntary basis. These standards are intended to improve financial decision making, investor confidence, and global competitiveness.
