The President of India, as per the Constitution of India, has several important duties:

1. Executive Functions: The President is the head of the executive and appoints the Prime Minister and other members of Council of Ministers who are collectively responsible to the Lok Sabha (the lower house of Parliament).

2. Legislative Functions: The President summons and prorogues each House of Parliament, addresses both Houses at the beginning of the first session after each general election and the budget session, and for the purpose of addressing the nation at other times. He also has the power to dissolve the Lok Sabha.

3. Judicial Functions: The President can grant pardons, reprieves, respites or remissions of punishment in certain cases.

4. Diplomatic Functions: The President represents India in its relations with other countries and is the first citizen of India.

5. Emergency Powers: In times of internal disturbance or external aggression, the President can declare a state of emergency or martial law under certain conditions.

6. Financial Functions: The President has the power to open, close, or adjourn each session of Parliament, and to specify the time within which the Houses shall meet.

7. Appointment Functions: The President appoints Governors of States, Attorney General of India, Chief Election Commissioner, Comptroller and Auditor General of India, members of Union Public Service Commission, Ambassadors, High Commissioners, and other diplomats to foreign nations, and the Chief Justice of India and other judges of the Supreme Court.

8. Military Functions: The President is the Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces of the Union.

9. Veto Powers: The President has the power to return a bill for reconsideration, which can be passed again with or without amendments by a two-thirds majority in both Houses. If the same bill is sent again, the President is bound to give assent.

10. Emergency Provisions: The President can exercise certain powers during an emergency such as assuming to himself all or any of the functions of any ministry or department of state, and exercising his executive authority through an administrator appointed by him.
