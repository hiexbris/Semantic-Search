1. SpaceX was founded by Elon Musk in 2002 with an initial investment of $180 million from his PayPal stocks.
2. The first and second employees were Tom Mueller and Chris Thompson.
3. SpaceX's primary goal is to decrease the cost and improve the reliability of access to space, ultimately by a factor of ten.
4. In 2006, SpaceX unveiled its spacecraft under the name Dragon.
5. SpaceX developed the Falcon 9, which costs significantly less than traditional NASA rockets to develop.
6. As of 2024, SpaceX operates a Rideshare and Bandwagon (mid inclination) programs for small satellite launches.
7. COO Gwynne Shotwell and vice president Mark Juncosa oversee Starbase, SpaceX's Texas launch facility.
8. SpaceX is not publicly traded; a space industry newspaper estimated that SpaceX has a revenue of over $10 billion in 2024.
