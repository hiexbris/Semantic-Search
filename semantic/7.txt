1. Metformin should be taken after a meal to reduce stomach side effects like nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or gas.
2. The most serious side effect of metformin is lactic acidosis, a potentially life-threatening condition characterized by the buildup of lactic acid in the bloodstream.
3. Metformin may interact with other medications such as those managing blood pressure, seizures, heartburn, and chlorostrol.
4. Metformin can decrease vitamin B12 levels in your body, which may cause anemia in rare cases.
5. Early signs of hypoglycemia include shakiness, sweating, fast heartbeat, and anxiety. If you combine metformin with insulin or other diabetes medications that raise your risk for hypoglycemia, you may develop hypoglycemia.
6. Metformin is generally considered safe during pregnancy or nursing, either by itself or with insulin.
7. In rare cases, it's possible to have a serious allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) to metformin, characterized by a swollen, raised, itchy, blistered, or peeling rash.
