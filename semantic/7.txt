1. Mild gastrointestinal side effects: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, and loss of appetite.
2. Other common side effects: Metallic taste, headache, and a feeling of weakness or dizziness.
3. Long-term use risk: Vitamin B12 deficiency, which can cause symptoms such as extreme tiredness, breathlessness, and faintness.
4. Rare but severe side effects: Lactic acidosis (a life-threatening condition caused by a pH imbalance and too much lactic acid in the bloodstream). This requires immediate medical treatment.
5. Allergic reaction symptoms: Rash that is swollen, raised, itchy, blistered or peeling; anaphylaxis.
6. Note for anyone concerned about vitamin B12 deficiency: Foods high in vitamin B12 and supplementation can help boost levels. It's important to follow a healthy meal plan and have food in your stomach while taking metformin.
