1. Saudi Arabia is the largest economy in the Arab world, with a GDP of over $1.1 trillion nominally and more than $2.3 trillion by purchasing power parity (PPP).

2. The country has the second largest proven reserves of petroleum in the world, making it the third largest producer and the largest exporter. It also has the sixth-largest proven natural gas reserves.

3. The private sector in Saudi Arabia is dominated by a handful of big businesses in the service sector, primarily in construction and real estate. Some notable companies include Olayan, Zamil, Almarai, Mobily, STC, SABIC, Sadara, Halliburton, Baker Hughes, Flynas, Hilton, Zain, Yanbu Cement, Alhokair, MBC, Mahfouz, Al Rajhi, and Alfanar.

4. Saudi Arabia is a notable destination country for men and women trafficked for the purposes of slave labor and sexual exploitation.

5. The kingdom has one of the fastest 5G internet speeds in the world and is the 27th largest market for e-commerce with a revenue of US$8 billion in 2021. As of 2020, 98% of the population are internet users.

6. The Saudi Arabian cuisine is similar to that of the surrounding countries in the Arabian Peninsula and the wider Arab world, and has influenced and been influenced by Turkish, Indian, Persian, and African food. Islamic dietary laws are enforced: pork is not allowed, and other animals are slaughtered in accordance with halal.
