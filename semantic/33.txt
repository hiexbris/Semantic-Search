The principles of Agile software development, as outlined in the Agile Manifesto, are:

1. Customer Satisfaction: Deliver working software frequently to customers so they can provide feedback and value is delivered quickly.
2. Collaboration: Business people and developers must work together daily throughout the project.
3. Adaptability: Respond to change over following a plan. Agile processes harness change for the customer's competitive advantage.
4. Continuous Improvement: Simplify procedures, amplify working arrangements, and promote continuous improvement.
5. Technical Excellence: Good design enhances agility.
6. Sustainable Development: Maintain a constant pace indefinitely.
7. Early Delivery: Working software is the primary measure of progress.
8. Continuous Integration: Frequently integrate code changes to maintain a potentially releasable working system.
9. Simplicity: The art of maximizing the amount of work not done - is essential.
10. Self-Organization: Encourage self-organization and technical empowerment at all levels.
11. Face-to-Face Conversation: Facilitate face-to-face conversation whenever possible.
12. Continuous Reflection: Regularly reflect on how to become more effective, then tune and adjust.
