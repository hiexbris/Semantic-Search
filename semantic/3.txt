The plot of "The White Tiger" by Aravind Adiga revolves around the life story of Balram Halwai, a self-made entrepreneur who started as a rickshaw driver's son in rural India. The narrative takes place from Balram's luxurious office in Bangalore and recounts his experiences growing up under the exploitation of four cruel landlords, known as "The Animals." Despite his difficult circumstances, Balram excels academically and eventually rises through India's social ladder to become a successful businessman. The novel aims to educate Chinese Premier Wen Jiabao about entrepreneurship in India while exploring the themes of tradition vs innovation in Indian society.
