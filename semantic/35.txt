1. An NRI, PIO, or OCI should file an Income Tax Return (ITR) in India if their total annual income exceeds the specified threshold.
2. The taxable income for this purpose includes gross total income before giving effect to exemption on re-investment of capital gains and Chapter VI-A deductions such as donations, investment in life insurance policy/ Unit Linked Insurance Policy/Equity Linked Savings Scheme, Mediclaim etc.
3. The rules and deadlines for filing ITR are similar to that of resident Indians.
4. It is beneficial to file an ITR voluntarily in many ways, but mandatory only under certain circumstances.
5. Regular consultation with a tax expert is recommended to ensure adherence to Indian tax regulations and keep abreast of the latest NRI income tax filing rules.
