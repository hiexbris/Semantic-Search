1. The President of India executes various constitutional duties, including giving assent to bills, appointing key officials, and overseeing emergency provisions when required. (Executive Powers)
2. The President upholds, protects, and defends the Constitution, ensuring that laws are enacted and enforced according to constitutional provisions. (Legislative Power)
3. The President has no judicial powers or functions.
4. The President is the symbol of the Nation and serves as a ceremonial head. (Symbolic Role)
5. The President wields various supervisory powers over the executive and legislative entities of India to uphold the constitution. (Supervisory Powers)
6. There is no bar on the actions of the president to contest in the court of law. (Legal Contestability)
