1. The Lokpal Act mandates public servants to declare their assets and liabilities, including those of their spouses and dependent children.
2. These declarations must be filed by July 31st every year and published on the Ministry's website by August 31st.
3. The Act seeks to establish an institution (Lokpal) to inquire into allegations of corruption against certain public functionaries, such as the Prime Minister, cabinet ministers, members of parliament, Group A officials of the Central Government.
4. In 2014, a Bill was proposed to amend the Lokpal Act, aiming to modify the provision related to declaration of assets by public servants. The new provision would require public servants to declare all their assets and liabilities in the prescribed manner by the central government. (If passed)
